The problem

Most child safety wearables are built for the wrong environment.

GPS trackers are designed for open ground. They struggle exactly where parents need them most — indoors, in dense crowds, underground, or anywhere satellite signal is unreliable.

01

GPS drifts indoors

Malls, fairs, subway platforms, and stadiums all degrade GPS accuracy to the point where a "location" can be fifty meters off — too slow and too vague in the moment a child actually wanders.

02

Screens invite distraction

A kids' smartwatch with apps and games becomes its own babysitting problem. Parents end up managing a second device rather than feeling reassured by one.

03

Health signals are an afterthought

Most kids trackers stop at location. None combine proximity alerts with the vitals that matter for a young child: heart rate, oxygen, and sleep quality.

The product

One soft band. Four things a parent actually needs to know.

No screen, no apps for the child to learn, no games to fight over. Just a woven, all-day-comfortable band that quietly does its job.

Woven textile band · soft sky-blue lining · five-dot status light · no screen

◎

Proximity alerts, parent-defined

The band stays linked to a parent's phone over Bluetooth. The parent sets the safe radius — tighter at a fairground, looser at the park — and gets an instant alert the moment that radius is crossed. No GPS lag, no satellite dependency.

♥

Continuous heart rate

An optical sensor on the underside reads heart rate throughout the day, flagging readings outside a healthy range for young children so a stress event or overexertion doesn't go unnoticed.

☾

Sleep quality tracking

Motion and heart rate together build a picture of how well a child actually slept — not just hours in bed, but restlessness and disruption, surfaced as a simple nightly summary for parents.

◐

Blood oxygen monitoring

The same sensor cluster reads SpO₂, giving an early signal for breathing difficulty — particularly valuable for families managing asthma or other respiratory concerns.

How it works

Built for the moment a crowd gets between a parent and a child.

Bluetooth proximity sensing, smoothed against false alarms, with an optional GPS snapshot the instant something needs a second look.

i

Set the radius

Parent picks a safe distance in the app — tighter for crowds, looser for open space.

ii

Band stays linked

A constant low-power Bluetooth signal tracks distance from the paired phone in real time.

iii

Threshold confirmed

Multiple readings confirm the crossing, so a crowded room doesn't trigger a false alert.

iv

Parent is notified

Band vibrates, phone alerts instantly, with an optional GPS pin dropped at that moment.
