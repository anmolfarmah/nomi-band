The features

One soft band. Four things a parent actually needs to know.

No screen, no apps for the child to learn, no games to fight over. Just a woven, all-day-comfortable band that quietly does its job.

Woven textile band · soft sky-blue lining · five-dot status light · no screen

◎

Proximity alerts, parent-defined

The band stays linked to a parent's phone over Bluetooth. The parent sets the safe radius and gets an instant alert the moment that radius is crossed — built for the exact moment a crowd gets between a parent and a child.

♥

Continuous heart rate

A sensor on the underside reads heart rate throughout the day, flagging readings outside a healthy range for young children so a stress event or overexertion doesn't go unnoticed.

☾

Sleep quality tracking

Motion and heart rate together build a picture of how well a child actually slept — restlessness and disruption, surfaced as a simple nightly summary for parents.

◐

Blood oxygen monitoring

The same sensor reads SpO₂, giving an early signal for breathing difficulty — particularly useful for families managing asthma or other respiratory concerns.

⚑

One-press help signal

A recessed button under the band sends an immediate alert to the parent's phone when held — simple enough for a young child to use, hard enough to press by accident.

How it works

Built for the moment a crowd gets between a parent and a child.

Proximity sensing that works indoors, outdoors, and everywhere GPS struggles — smoothed against false alarms.

i

Set the range

A parent picks a safe distance — tighter for crowds, looser for open space.

ii

Band stays linked

A constant low-power signal tracks distance from the paired phone in real time.

iii

Crossing confirmed

Multiple readings confirm the crossing, so a crowded room doesn't trigger a false alert.

iv

Parent is notified

The band gives a gentle pulse, the phone alerts instantly, and a location can be dropped in if needed.

What's inside

Real sensing hardware, sized down to fit a child's wrist.

Nothing experimental — the same categories of sensors used in today's fitness wearables, chosen and tuned for a much smaller, much more active wearer.

♥
Optical heart & oxygen sensor

A small light-based sensor sits flush against the wrist to read both heart rate and blood oxygen from the same contact point.

↻
Motion sensor

Tracks movement and stillness to recognize activity, steps, and sleep stages through the night.

◎
Low-power wireless link

Keeps a constant, battery-friendly connection to the parent's phone for distance sensing.

⚡
Multi-day battery

Designed to comfortably outlast a full day of play between charges, with a simple charging cradle.

⬡
Woven, washable strap

A breathable textile band built to handle sweat, splashes, and the general wear of active kids.

✺
Light, not a screen

A simple five-point indicator communicates status at a glance, without inviting a child to stare at a display.

The parent experience

The band does the watching. The app does the explaining.

Everything a parent sets and sees lives in a simple companion app — the child never needs to touch it.

01

Set it once

Choose a safe distance for the day's activity and pair the band — a one-time setup before heading out.

02

Check in anytime

A quiet, glanceable view of current distance, heart rate, and connection status, without needing to ask the child anything.

03

Look back later

A simple nightly summary of sleep quality and the day's activity, so patterns are easy to notice over time.
